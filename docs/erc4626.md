# ERC-4626 accounting

`VestingEscrow4626` vests principal measured in the vault's underlying asset
while holding and transferring vault shares. This distinction lets yield go to
a fixed destination without making the standard-token escrow depend on vault
logic.

## Units

The API uses two explicit unit suffixes:

| Name | Unit |
| --- | --- |
| `funded_shares` | Vault shares |
| `principal_assets` | Underlying assets |
| `claimed_principal_assets` | Underlying assets |
| `claimable_principal_assets()` | Underlying assets |
| `max_principal_assets` | Underlying assets |
| `claimable_yield_shares()` | Vault shares |
| `claim_principal()` return value | Vault shares |
| `claim_yield()` return value | Vault shares |

Do not treat the return value of `claim_principal()` as the principal asset
amount. Use its `PrincipalClaim` event or call `preview_principal_claim()` when
both values are needed.

## Initialization

The factory transfers exactly `funded_shares` into the proxy. Initialization
then records:

```text
principal_assets = vault.convertToAssets(funded_shares)
```

That asset amount is the fixed vesting schedule principal. It is not updated
when the vault's share price changes.

Initialization rejects:

- zero funded shares;
- zero asset value;
- principal above `2**128 - 1`;
- an `asset()` result that is not a contract;
- conversions where `convertToShares(principal_assets)` is zero or greater
  than the funded shares;
- a zero, proxy, vault, or underlying asset address as `yield_recipient`.

These checks catch basic incompatibilities but do not replace review of the
specific vault.

## Principal and yield split

At any point, let:

- `R` be remaining principal entitlement in asset units;
- `B` be the escrow's vault share balance;
- `V = vault.convertToAssets(B)` be the current value of that balance.

If `V <= R`, the contract treats every share as principal and reports zero
yield. This is the loss case.

If `V > R`, the contract starts from `vault.convertToShares(R)` and adds one raw
share when converting that result back to assets would leave the principal
reserve below `R`. The remaining balance is yield:

```text
principal shares = convertToShares(R), rounded up when necessary
yield shares     = B - principal shares
```

`claim_yield()` transfers only the yield side of this split. It therefore
cannot consume the currently required principal reserve under the supported
conversion model.

Directly transferred shares are not tracked by provenance. They restore a
principal shortfall first and become claimable yield only when the escrow's
value exceeds remaining principal.

## Principal claims

Let:

- `A` be the smaller of currently vested, unclaimed principal and
  `max_principal_assets`;
- `Q` be the current principal share allocation;
- `R` be remaining principal assets before the claim.

The contract reserves the post-claim fraction with upward rounding:

```text
principal after = R - A
reserved shares = ceil(Q * (R - A) / R)
payout shares   = Q - reserved shares
```

It then increases `claimed_principal_assets` by `A` and transfers the payout
shares. Upward reserve rounding prevents repeated partial claims from gradually
eating into the share reserve.

When `A == R`, all principal shares are paid. Any separately identified yield
shares remain for `yield_recipient`.

## Gains

When the vault share value rises:

- the original asset-denominated principal continues to vest on schedule;
- fewer shares are needed to reserve the remaining principal;
- excess shares become permissionlessly claimable to `yield_recipient`;
- principal claims transfer the shares corresponding to the recipient's asset
  entitlement at the current conversion rate.

The yield recipient is fixed at initialization. Neither the recipient, revoker,
nor caller can redirect yield.

## Losses

When the total share value falls to or below remaining principal:

- `claimable_yield_shares()` returns zero;
- all shares are treated as principal;
- a principal claim receives its proportional share of the depleted reserve;
- `claimed_principal_assets` still advances by the asset-denominated schedule
  entitlement consumed by that claim.

This avoids freezing the escrow when the vault cannot make principal whole.
The economic loss is borne proportionally by all outstanding principal rather
than by whichever party claims last.

## Revocation

Revocation uses the same split and proportional payout:

1. Compute principal vested at the current block timestamp.
2. Split the current share balance into principal and yield.
3. Transfer the unvested fraction of principal shares to the explicit
   revocation receiver.
4. Transfer current yield shares to `yield_recipient`.
5. Leave the vested, unclaimed principal shares in the escrow.
6. Freeze vesting and clear the revoker.

If the vault is impaired, the vested and unvested sides share the loss
proportionally. The recipient can claim its retained side after revocation.
Subsequent gains on the retained shares continue to accrue to
`yield_recipient`.

## Rounding

ERC-4626 conversions normally round down. The escrow counters this when
reserving principal by adding a share if the first conversion does not cover
the required asset amount, and it rounds the post-claim reserve upward.

With reviewed Yearn-style vaults and conventional share precision, a lifecycle
transition can differ from the ideal fractional allocation by less than one raw
share. This is deliberate: the contract does not maintain cross-call rounding
checkpoints.

Vaults with coarse share precision can turn a one-share bound into meaningful
value and are outside the supported deployment policy.

## Vault requirements

A candidate vault must be reviewed for all of the following:

- `asset()`, `convertToAssets()`, and `convertToShares()` conform to ERC-4626;
- conversions are monotonic and use conventional floor rounding;
- one additional raw share is sufficient to cover a conversion-rounding
  shortfall;
- share transfers move the exact requested balance and do not rebase;
- the vault cannot selectively block the escrow, recipient, revoker, or yield
  recipient;
- pauses, shutdowns, or upgrades cannot unexpectedly prevent share transfers;
- share precision makes the documented rounding bound economically negligible;
- oracle-free conversion functions cannot be manipulated within a lifecycle
  transaction in a way the deployment policy considers unacceptable.

The repository's integration smoke test exercises sUSDS at a pinned mainnet
block, but passing that test is not approval of every ERC-4626 implementation.
